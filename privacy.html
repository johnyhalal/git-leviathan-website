Privacy Policy

Last updated: 7 July 2026

GitLeviathan is a desktop application for working with Git repositories. It runs entirely on your own computer. This policy explains what data the app and this website handle, and what they do not.

The short version

GitLeviathan does not collect, store, or transmit your personal information to us. We do not run analytics, we do not use tracking cookies, and we have no servers that receive your data. Your repositories, credentials, and activity stay on your machine and go only to the Git hosting services you choose to connect to.

Data the app stores locally

To function, GitLeviathan keeps the following on your device, using your operating system's standard storage locations:

  • Application settings — such as your theme preference and window state.
  • Account credentials and access tokens for the Git services you connect (for example GitHub or GitLab). Where the platform supports it, these are held in your operating system's secure credential store.
  • Local clones of your repositories and their Git history, in the folders you choose.

This data never leaves your device except when you ask the app to communicate with a Git host (for example, to clone, fetch, pull, or push).

Third-party services you connect

When you sign in to a Git hosting provider or clone from a Git URL, GitLeviathan communicates directly with that service on your behalf. Your use of those services is governed by their own privacy policies, not ours:

  • GitHub Privacy Statement
  • GitLab Privacy Statement

We do not sit between you and these providers, and we do not receive a copy of anything you send to or from them.

Downloads and updates

Application downloads and release files are hosted on GitHub. When you download GitLeviathan or check for a new version, your request goes to GitHub, whose data handling is covered by the GitHub privacy statement linked above.

This website

This website is a static download page. It does not set tracking cookies and does not run its own analytics. Depending on how it is hosted, the hosting provider may keep standard server access logs (such as IP addresses and request times) for security and operational purposes.

Children's privacy

GitLeviathan is a developer tool and is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.

Contact

If you have questions about this policy, please open an issue on the GitLeviathan GitHub repository.
